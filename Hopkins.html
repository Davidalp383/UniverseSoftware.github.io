Johns Hopkins University

It is one of the top universities in the United States, ranked among the top 25 worldwide. Its quality and excellence enable advancements in scientific fields, leading to remarkable outcomes in its environment. The key to its success over its 145-year history lies in its teaching and education strategies. Academic achievements, both as a university community and at an individual level, are cultivated through ambitious programs designed to maintain its global leadership. Johns Hopkins University retains the pioneering spirit that led it to revolutionize the higher education system when it became the first research university in the country.

As one of the early research-focused universities, Johns Hopkins University promotes globalized thinking for professional development. This means it encourages students to tackle global-scale issues. Scientific careers, including computer engineering, are emphasized, receiving consistent support from the institution for students to initiate research for the application of technology and systems on a global scale. The university also encourages exploration in various fields such as medicine, genetics, veterinary science, physics, etc., supporting these areas for human development.
