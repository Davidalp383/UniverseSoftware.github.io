California Institute of Technology

"California Institute of Technology," commonly known as Caltech, is a private university located in Pasadena, United States. It is renowned for the quality of its studies in natural sciences and engineering and is consistently ranked as one of the best universities in the world.

Its six divisions—biology, chemistry and chemical engineering, engineering and applied science, geological and planetary sciences, humanities and social sciences, and physics, mathematics, and astronomy— collectively host around 900 undergraduate students and approximately 1200 doctoral students.

Caltech oversees and operates the Jet Propulsion Laboratory for the United States space agency, NASA. Even before graduation, students at this university have the opportunity to intern in the development of software and hardware systems for the international space program (NASA) or in the advancement of technologies for space travel and extraplanetary exploration.

Many professionals who graduate from this university often work for NASA in various programs related to the development of technologies and systems or are involved in developing operating systems for the United States Air Force.
