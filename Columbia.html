Columbia University

Officially known as Columbia University in the City of New York, or simply Columbia, it is a private American university located in Upper Manhattan, New York. It is a member of the Ivy League and is one of the most prestigious and selective universities globally, with an admission rate of 3.66%. Founded in 1754, it is the oldest institution of higher education in the state of New York, the fifth oldest in the United States, and one of the nine colonial colleges founded before the Revolutionary War. It is recognized as one of the most prestigious universities in the world.

Among the most sought-after programs at this university are electronic engineering, systems engineering, and computer engineering. Due to the university's prestige in New York and the constant demand for professionals in these fields, the likelihood of practicing these professions in the city is very high, approximately 89%.
