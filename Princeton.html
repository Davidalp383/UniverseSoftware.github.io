Princeton University

Princeton University is one of the eight private research universities in the Ivy League, located in Princeton, New Jersey. Originally founded as the College of New Jersey in 1746 and initially located in Elizabeth, it was later moved to Princeton in 1756 while retaining its original name. The official name was changed to Princeton University in 1896. Originally a Presbyterian institution, it is currently non-denominational.

Ranking second and third as the most sought-after programs at this university, the majors in systems engineering and information sciences and computer science receive extensive support in research and the development of computer technologies. The university ensures that these programs receive the utmost support for cutting-edge research in software development and cybersecurity. This constant focus on research has made Princeton University one of the most awarded institutions, especially in Nobel Prizes in the fields of technology and systems.

Graduates from Princeton University are highly favored by major technology companies and social media giants such as Meta, Alphabet (Google), Microsoft, and others. Government entities in the United States, including NASA, the FBI, and the Pentagon, also prefer professionals who graduated from Princeton University. This preference is due to the university's deep emphasis on incorporating top-tier research capabilities in its graduates, keeping them abreast of the latest developments in technology and software.
